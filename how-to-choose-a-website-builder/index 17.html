How to choose a website builder for a small business.

The best website builder is not just the one with the most buttons. It is the one that helps you publish a useful, trustworthy website without getting lost.

Start with PBI View examples

Check the templates match real businesses

Good templates should fit the kind of business you run, not just look pretty in a demo.

Look for support beyond design

Domains, SEO, images, wording, forms, payments and publishing are often where people get stuck.

Make sure editing feels controllable

You should be able to change colours, layouts, backgrounds, images, buttons and key page content without technical guesswork.

Think about what happens after launch

A website needs updates. Choose a builder that gives you a route for improvement, support and search-friendly content.

FAQs

Common questions

Should I choose the cheapest builder?

Not automatically. Cheap is only useful if you can finish and maintain the site.

Do I need AI?

AI can help with wording and structure, but you still need clear business information and good judgement.

What should I test before paying?

Test templates, editing, mobile view, forms, publishing and support routes.
