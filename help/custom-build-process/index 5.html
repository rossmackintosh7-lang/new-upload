PBI custom build guide

What happens after a custom build enquiry?

A custom build is for customers who want more help than the standard builder or assisted setup.

Step 1: Enquiry review

PBI reviews the enquiry to understand the business type, goals, pages needed, design expectations, budget range and any time-sensitive launch requirements.

Step 2: Scope and recommendation

The project is shaped into clear work: what pages are needed, what content is missing, whether images are ready, whether retail/payment features are involved and what launch support is required.

Step 3: Quote or deposit

If the project is suitable, PBI confirms the next step. Some builds may start with a deposit so time can be reserved and the project can move into planning.

Step 4: Build and review

PBI prepares the website structure, wording and layout, then the customer reviews it. The review stage is for practical refinements, not endless shape-shifting into a dragon with a booking form.

Step 5: Launch

Once the site is checked, PBI helps with publishing, domain setup and basic launch checks. Ongoing improvements can then be handled through update support or SEO Care.

What makes a custom build smoother?

  • Clear business details.
  • Good images or a plan for sourcing them.
  • A realistic list of pages.
  • Known products, services, pricing or enquiry routes.
  • One main decision-maker for feedback.
