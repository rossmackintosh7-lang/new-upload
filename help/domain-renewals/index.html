PBI domain guide

Keep your domain active and under control.

A domain is rented, not owned forever. It must be renewed so your website and email do not unexpectedly stop working.

How domain renewals work

Domains are registered for a set period, usually one year at a time. When that period ends, the domain needs to be renewed. If it is not renewed, the website may stop loading and the domain could eventually become available to someone else.

If you manage your own domain

You remain responsible for renewal reminders, billing, DNS records and keeping access to the registrar account. This works well if you are comfortable managing domain settings.

If PBI helps manage the domain

PBI can help with domain setup, DNS changes and keeping the domain connected to the website. Any domain management fee is separate from the website subscription unless clearly bundled into your plan.

What to keep safe

  • Registrar login details.
  • Renewal dates and billing card details.
  • DNS records for website, email and verification services.
  • Proof of who controls the domain.

Before changing DNS

Do not delete DNS records blindly. Email, website hosting, Stripe, Resend, Google and other services may all use DNS records. One careless click can unplug a surprising number of wires.
