PBI content guide

Write a homepage that makes the next step obvious.

Your homepage should quickly explain who you help, what you offer and why someone should contact you.

The job of your homepage

Your homepage is not there to tell your whole life story. It should give visitors enough confidence to keep reading, click a service, make an enquiry or buy from you.

Use this simple structure

  1. Clear headline: say what you do in plain English.
  2. Short intro: explain who you help and what problem you solve.
  3. Main services: show the key things customers can book, buy or ask for.
  4. Trust points: add experience, location, reviews, photos, qualifications or proof.
  5. Next step: use one strong call-to-action, such as request a quote, book a call or view products.

Example homepage wording

Weak: “Welcome to our website. We are passionate about what we do.”

Better: “Reliable plumbing and heating support across Purbeck, from emergency repairs to bathroom fitting and boiler servicing.”

What to include

  • Your business name and location.
  • The main thing you want customers to do.
  • Your most important services or products.
  • What makes you useful, trustworthy or different.
  • Contact details or a clear button to enquire.

Good homepage wording should feel calm, direct and useful. No fog machine. No corporate soup. Just the customer finding the door handle quickly.
