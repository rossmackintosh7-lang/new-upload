PBI structure guide

Choose pages based on what customers need to know.

The best website structure is not the biggest one. It is the one that answers customer questions before they have to ask.

Start with the essentials

Most small business websites should start with a few strong pages rather than a maze of half-finished sections.

Home

A clear overview of what you do, who it is for and the main next step.

Services or Products

Show what customers can book, buy or ask about. Keep wording practical and specific.

About

Add the human side: who you are, where you work, your experience and why customers can trust you.

Contact

Make phone, email, enquiry forms, opening hours and service area easy to find.

Useful extra pages

  • Gallery: good for trades, food, salons, stays, events and visual work.
  • FAQs: useful when customers ask the same things before buying or booking.
  • Menu or Price guide: useful for hospitality, beauty and simple service businesses.
  • Testimonials: useful for building trust when customers are comparing options.
  • Retail shop: useful if you have products, vouchers, hampers or simple stock to sell.

Do not overbuild too early

If a page has no useful content yet, leave it out. A short, finished website is stronger than a large website full of empty rooms.
