PBI image guide

Use images that make the business feel real.

Good images help visitors understand the business quickly. They should support the wording, not distract from it.

What good website images do

Images should show the customer what kind of business they are dealing with. Real work, real places, real products and real details usually beat generic stock photos.

Best images to collect

  • Hero image: one strong image for the top of the page.
  • Service images: pictures that match your main services or products.
  • Trust images: team, workspace, shopfront, van, certificates, packaging or finished work.
  • Gallery images: before-and-after work, food, rooms, products or completed jobs.

Image rules that help

Use clear lighting

Natural, bright photos usually feel more honest than dark or over-filtered images.

Keep the subject obvious

Visitors should understand the image without having to decode it like a treasure map.

Avoid messy backgrounds

Clear spaces, tidy counters and finished work make the site feel more professional.

Check mobile view

An image that works on desktop may crop badly on mobile. Always check before publishing.

Using stock images

Stock images are fine when you need a temporary visual, but do not let them replace your business identity. PBI templates use polished example images, but your finished site should gradually move toward your own photos.
