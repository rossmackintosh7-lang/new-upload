PBI domain guide

Choose a domain customers can remember.

Your domain should be easy to say, easy to spell and closely linked to your business name or service.

What makes a good domain?

A good domain is not clever for the sake of it. It helps customers recognise you, trust you and find you again without having to search through old messages.

Keep it close to your business name

If your business is called Harbour Table, a domain like harbourtable.co.uk is much clearer than something vague or over-styled.

Make it easy to say out loud

If you would struggle to say it over the phone, it is probably too complicated. Avoid unnecessary hyphens, numbers and odd spellings.

Use the right ending

For most UK local businesses, .co.uk or .uk feels familiar. A .com can also work if it is available and fits your brand.

Think about local search

If your business depends on location, a local clue can help. For example, a trades business might use the town, area or county if the main name is already taken.

What to avoid

  • Long domains that customers are unlikely to type correctly.
  • Names that are too close to another business.
  • Hyphens unless they genuinely make the name clearer.
  • Words that limit your business if you may expand later.

Choosing between your own domain and a PBI subdomain

A PBI subdomain is fine for getting started quickly. A custom domain is better when you want the website to feel established, professional and easier to share on signs, menus, vans, invoices or social media.

Before publishing, check the spelling carefully. A domain is part of your business identity, so it should feel sturdy enough to put on a shopfront.
